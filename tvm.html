Thiruvananthapuram

Trivandrum or Thiruvananthapuram is the capital city of Kerala. Located on the western coastal regions of India, this metropolis is also the IT hub of God’s Own Country and one of the most sought after tourist destinations in Kerala.

Once cited as the ‘Evergreen City of India’ by Mahatma Gandhi, Trivandrum has a diverse history and is a home to some of the most colourful and striking cultures. During the ancient times, the city had served as a major spice trading point among several Asian countries.

The city is built across seven of the most captivating hills and is dotted with several pious temples and religious sites. Though the city witness a number of rulers in its past, it has endured its beauty and liveliness in the most splendid ways, and has become of the highest visited tourist destinations in Kerala.

Trivandrum (official name Thiruvananthapuram), lying on the southwest coast of India, is the capital to the state of Kerala. The place is well connected to most of the other major cities of India through rail, road, and air network. Because of its proximity to the sea, the climate of Trivandrum is tropical. This also gives Trivandrum a very pleasant weather round the year. The average maximum temperature can go up to 35.2 degree celsius in summer months and to a minimum of 18.0 degree celsius in the winters. Monsoon comes to Trivandrum in the month of May and remains until November.

How To Reach

By Bus

The capital city of Kerala enjoys a very decent connectivity with other major South Indian cities. Vsitors can reach Trivandrum easily by boarding direct buses from cities like Bangalore, Chennai, Ernakulam, Mumbai, Thrissur, and Kozhikode.

By Air

Trivandrum International Airport is around 6km from the heart of the city and is the nearest airport to reach Trivandrum. The airport hosts a large number of flights from some of the major Indian as well as international cities. After arriving at the airport, visitors can hire private taxis or other vehicles to reach Trivandrum without any hassles.

By Rail

Thiruvananthapuram has its own railway station which is well-connected to some of the major destinations across India. Trivandrum Central is also known to be the largest and the busiest railway station in the entire of Kerala.

Best Time to Visit


Winter
(Oct to Feb)


Trivandrum welcomes the winter with a drop in the temperature level and a pleasant ambience. Though the atmosphere remains quite chilly, the city experiences the highest influx of tourists during this season.


Summer
(Mar to May)


Summer brings in a rise in the mercury level in Trivandrum. Visiting this city during these three months might make the visitors exhausted as the climate gets hot and arid. It is not the best time to visit Trivandrum; however due to the reduction in tourism, visitors can enjoy a secluded holiday during the summers.


Monsoon
(Jun to Sep)


This is the best time for the nature lovers to plan for a Kerala trip and visit Trivandrum. With new and green sprouts everywhere, the city gets a new and alluring look during the monsoons.
